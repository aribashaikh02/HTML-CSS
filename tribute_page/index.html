Ada Lovelace

The Visionary Who Saw the Future of Computing

Timeline of a Pioneer

  • 1815 - Born in London, England to Lord Byron and Lady Byron.
  • 1828 - At just 12 years old, designed a flying machine based on mathematical studies of birds.
  • 1833 - Met Charles Babbage through their mutual tutor Mary Somerville, sparking a lifelong collaboration on the Analytical Engine.
  • 1843 - Published her extensive notes on Babbage's Analytical Engine, which included "Note G"—widely recognized as the world's first computer algorithm.
  • 1852 - Passed away at the young age of 36, leaving a legacy that wouldn't be fully appreciated until the digital age.

"The Analytical Engine weaves algebraic patterns just as the Jacquard loom weaves flowers and leaves."

— Ada Lovelace
